Interests

My interests are as follows:

  • Computer Architecture
  • FPGAs
  • Reconfigurable Computing
  • Deep Neural Networks
  • Hardware Accelerators
  • Approximate Computing

The high computation demanded by deep learning models motivated the hardware acceleration of Neural Networks. Recent studies have focused on four architectures of accelerators for DNNs, including GPU, FPGA. ASIC, and CGRA.
Because of the high power consumption of GPU, it's not efficient to use them in embedded systems, as in these devices power consumption is a critical challenge. Also, according to the development of Neural Networks, flexibility is the major benefit of FPGAs over the ASIC. Thanks to Deep Neural Network's inherent tolerance against errors, the bit-width of data and operations in Neural Networks can be reduced with a negligible impact on accuracy. In many designed accelerators, they have considered a minimum bit-width that preserves the original accuracy. However, researches have shown that not also the minimum required bit-width varies significantly across variant Neural Networks, but also it differs across different layers of a single Neural Network.
In my master thesis, my aim is to design an architecture for CNNs, which is able to change the working bit-width dynamically. The design supports run-time splitting an operator into multiple independent operators with smaller bit-width, effectively increasing throughput when lower precision is required. This architecture is designed for FPGA and the multipliers and bit-width adaption mechanism are optimized for the LUT-based structure of FPGAs.

Parallel and Network Processing Group (NOC Lab)

Network on Chip Laboratory is one of the laboratories of the School of Electrical and Computer Engineering of University of Tehran. The research group primarily focused on the exploration of high-performance and low-power computer architectures with a current emphasis on highly parallel computer architectures, interconnection networks, and in-memory processing. Recently, our team has been working on Neural Network Accelerators and Data/Computation Reuse for Efficient CNN Accelerators.
In this laboratory, I’m working on the implementation of an FPGA based multi-precision accelerator for deep neural networks. This architecture is able to dynamically change the working bit-width according to the minimum bit-width required to preserve the original accuracy. The multipliers and bit-width adaption mechanism are optimized for the LUT-based structure of FPGAs. All aforementioned researches are being supervised by Dr. Mehdi Modarressi
